Otter.ai dominated the "AI meeting notes" category from 2019 through 2024. Its Zoom/Meet/Teams integration was clean, the shared workspace useful, and the pricing sane at 17 $/mo Pro. But by 2026, the category has exploded and Otter is no longer the obvious default for everyone.

Here's who should stay, and who should switch, and to what.

## The Otter strengths that still hold

Let's be honest about what Otter still does best:

- **Bot-based meeting join**. You invite Otter@otter.ai, it hops into the call as a participant, transcribes, and posts the transcript to your workspace. No software install, works with any platform.
- **Shared team workspace**. Transcripts and summaries are collaboratively editable. Great for sales teams keeping CRM notes, HR doing panel interviews.
- **Search-across-meetings**. Type "pricing objection" in the search bar, get every moment across every meeting where someone said that.
- **Live captions during a call**. Useful for the hearing-impaired and for non-native speakers.

If those four bullets describe exactly your workflow, stay on Otter. Nothing below will serve you better.

## Where Otter falls short

- **Dictation**. Otter is not a dictation app. You can't dictate an email into Gmail via Otter. The only "input" is meeting audio. If you want voice-driven writing, look elsewhere.
- **Translation**. Otter transcribes in one language at a time. No real-time translation, no bilingual captions, no voice-to-voice.
- **Latency**. Otter's real-time captions lag 1.5-3 seconds behind the speaker. Fine for reading, useless for live translation.
- **Desktop-first workflows**. Otter is fundamentally a web app. The Mac / Windows apps are shells around the browser experience.
- **Pricing cliff**. Pro at 17 $ is reasonable, Business at 30 $/user/month starts to add up, and the Enterprise tier is "contact sales".

## 5 alternatives worth considering

### 1. VoiceInk — multi-purpose, desktop-native

- **Price**: Free / 9.90 €/mo Pro
- **Replaces**: Otter for dictation and translation. Not meeting notes (yet).

VoiceInk is a desktop pill that dictates, translates, and synthesises voice in under 400 ms. It shines when you're the one doing the talking — emails, documents, Discord calls, reports. The Listener feature captures incoming audio and transcribes it, which overlaps with Otter's meeting use case, but VoiceInk doesn't (yet) auto-join Zoom bots.

Switch if: you're paying Otter to transcribe your own talking, not group meetings.

### 2. Fireflies.ai — the direct Otter competitor

- **Price**: 10 $/mo Pro, 19 $/mo Business
- **Replaces**: Otter for meeting notes

Fireflies has feature parity with Otter on meeting transcription, a cleaner UI, and slightly cheaper pricing. The "AI Soundbites" feature auto-extracts clips by topic, which is great for marketing teams reusing customer quotes. Weaknesses: smaller team-workspace ecosystem than Otter, less mature on Zoom edge cases.

### 3. Krisp Notes — privacy-focused, on-device

- **Price**: 16 $/mo Pro
- **Replaces**: Otter for sensitive meetings (legal, medical, HR)

Krisp runs its noise-cancellation and transcription **on your device**, never uploading audio. For regulated industries (HIPAA, GDPR-heavy), it's the only serious option. Drawback: lacks the shared team workspace Otter has.

### 4. Granola — founder / PM favorite

- **Price**: 19 $/mo
- **Replaces**: Otter for solo founders / solopreneurs

Granola takes a radical UX bet: you still take rough notes during the meeting (or pretend to), and Granola's AI refines them post-call into polished summaries. It's the only tool that acknowledges you sometimes want to be ACTIVE in a meeting, not passive. Excellent for product managers, sales leaders, founders.

### 5. Tactiq — Google Meet native

- **Price**: Free for 10 meetings / mo, 12 $/mo Pro
- **Replaces**: Otter if you're Google-only

Tactiq is a Chrome extension that lives inside Google Meet. No bot joining. It captures the built-in Meet captions and augments them with AI summaries post-call. Cheap, fast, works. Limit: Google Meet exclusively.
